02

The Golden Ratio

Both the icosahedron and the dodecahedron are coordinated by the golden ratio:

$$\varphi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2} \approx 1.6180339887$$

Icosahedron vertices (12)

Cyclic permutations of:

$$(0,\; \pm 1,\; \pm \varphi)$$

Dodecahedron vertices (20)

$$(\pm 1, \pm 1, \pm 1), \quad (0, \pm \varphi^{-1}, \pm \varphi), \quad (\pm \varphi^{-1}, \pm \varphi, 0), \quad (\pm \varphi, 0, \pm \varphi^{-1})$$
Implementation
Three.js's IcosahedronGeometry and DodecahedronGeometry handle the $\varphi$-based coordinates internally; the implementation only consumes the resulting vertex buffers.
03

Neutral — Cumulated Icosahedron

The neutral form is a face-cumulation of the regular icosahedron: a pyramid is raised on every triangular face. (Colloquially “stellated,” though the classical stellations of the icosahedron use larger, non-arbitrary apex heights.)

Construction

  1. Start with THREE.IcosahedronGeometry(1, 0) — 12 vertices, 20 equilateral faces.
  2. Deduplicate vertices: positions are rounded to 5 decimals and collapsed through a hash map.
  3. For each face $(\vec{v}_1, \vec{v}_2, \vec{v}_3)$, compute:
    • Centroid: $\vec{c} = \tfrac{1}{3}(\vec{v}_1 + \vec{v}_2 + \vec{v}_3)$
    • Outward normal: $\hat{n} = \widehat{(\vec{v}_2 - \vec{v}_1) \times (\vec{v}_3 - \vec{v}_1)}$, negated if $\hat{n} \cdot \vec{c} < 0$
    • Apex: $\vec{a} = \vec{c} + h\,\hat{n}$, where $h = 0.3$
  4. Replace each face with three triangles: $(\vec{v}_1, \vec{v}_2, \vec{a})$, $(\vec{v}_2, \vec{v}_3, \vec{a})$, $(\vec{v}_3, \vec{v}_1, \vec{a})$.

Result: $20 \times 3 = 60$ triangular faces, packed into a Float32Array on a BufferGeometry.

04

Yes — Regular Octahedron

The yes form is the simplest of the three — a Platonic solid with no embellishment:

new THREE.OctahedronGeometry(1.2, 0)
  • Vertices: 6, at $(\pm 1.2, 0, 0)$, $(0, \pm 1.2, 0)$, $(0, 0, \pm 1.2)$
  • Faces: 8 equilateral triangles
  • Edges: 12
  • Symmetry group: $O_h$, order 48

Euler characteristic:

$$V - E + F = 6 - 12 + 8 = 2 \quad \checkmark$$

Its smooth, low-poly silhouette — rounded against the spiky neutral and no forms — reads as affirmative.

05

No — Cumulated Dodecahedron

The no form cumulates each of the dodecahedron's 12 pentagonal faces with a tall, varied spike.

Reconstructing pentagons

Three.js triangulates each pentagonal face into sub-triangles. The construction first groups them back into pentagons by testing pairs of triangles:

  • Normals nearly parallel: $\hat{n}_1 \cdot \hat{n}_2 > 0.95$
  • Centroids close: $\lVert \vec{c}_1 - \vec{c}_2 \rVert < 1.2$

Apex placement

For each pentagonal group $g$:

  1. Average all vertex positions to get the pentagon center $\vec{c}_g$.
  2. Take the outward normal $\hat{n}_g$ from the first sub-triangle (negate if $\hat{n}_g \cdot \vec{c}_g < 0$).
  3. Sample a spike height from the seeded hash; build spikes from each sub-triangle to the apex.

Spike heights

$$h_g = h_0 \,(0.8 + r_g), \qquad h_0 = 1.8, \quad r_g \in [0, 1)$$ $$h_g \in [1.44,\; 3.24]$$

where $r_g$ is a deterministic pseudo-random value seeded per face by $7g + 3$:

function seededRandom(seed) {
  var x = Math.sin(seed * 127.1 + seed * 311.7) * 43758.5453;
  return x - Math.floor(x);
}
Note
This is a degenerate version of the canonical Quilez GPU hash. Because both terms multiply the same seed, it algebraically collapses to $\sin(438.8\,s) \cdot 43758.5453$ — a 1D hash. The true trick takes a 2D input: dot(uv, vec2(127.1, 311.7)). For a single-input hash this is fine; the values are uncorrelated enough for spike-height variation.

Animation

Rotation

Each frame, the mesh advances on two axes:

$$\theta_y \mathrel{+}= \omega, \qquad \theta_x \mathrel{+}= 0.4\,\omega$$
State$\omega$ (rad/frame)
neutral0.008

The $y$ axis rotates 2.5× faster than the $x$ axis, producing an asymmetric tumble.

Float

A sinusoidal vertical displacement gives Bit a hovering quality:

$$y(t) = 0.15 \sin(1.5\,t)$$

Amplitude $0.15$ units, period $T = \tfrac{2\pi}{1.5} \approx 4.19$ s, with $t$ from a THREE.Clock.

07

Dual Polyhedra

The icosahedron and dodecahedron are dual polyhedra: faces and vertices of one correspond to vertices and faces of the other, with identical edge count and shared symmetry group $I_h$ (order 120).

SolidVEF
Icosahedron123020
Dodecahedron203012

Bit oscillates between cumulations of these duals: neutral cumulates the icosahedron, no cumulates the dodecahedron. The binary character literally moves between two dual geometric forms.

08

Information

Bit encodes exactly one bit of Shannon entropy. Under equal-probability yes / no:

$$H = -\sum_{i} p_i \log_2 p_i = -\left(\tfrac{1}{2}\log_2 \tfrac{1}{2} + \tfrac{1}{2}\log_2 \tfrac{1}{2}\right) = 1 \text{ bit}$$

This is the irreducible minimum of meaningful information — one binary decision — and Bit is its physical embodiment.

Lighting & Rendering

Material

MeshPhongMaterial with:

  • flatShading: true — per-face normals (no smoothing across vertices)
  • shininess: 80 — moderately sharp specular highlights
  • opacity: 0.92 — slight translucency for a holographic feel
  • side: DoubleSide — both faces of every triangle rendered

Phong reflection

Per-fragment color:

$$I = k_a I_a + \sum_{j} \left[ k_d (\hat{n} \cdot \hat{l}_j) I_j + k_s (\hat{r}_j \cdot \hat{v})^{\alpha} I_j \right]$$

with $k_a, k_d, k_s$ the ambient / diffuse / specular coefficients, $\hat{n}$ the face normal, $\hat{l}_j$ the light direction, $\hat{r}_j$ the reflection vector, $\hat{v}$ the view direction, and $\alpha = 80$ the shininess exponent.
